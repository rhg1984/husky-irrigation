Underground utilities — your responsibility for private lines

This is the most important section in these Terms. Please read it carefully.

Before any digging or trenching, Husky calls 811 (Texas811) to have public utilities marked — gas main, electric, water, telecom, and cable lines that belong to a utility company. This service is free and required by Texas law.

However, 811 only marks public utilities up to the meter. Texas811 does not mark private lines on your property. You are responsible for identifying and marking private utility lines before we begin work. Private lines include, but are not limited to:

  • Gas lines running to a propane tank, pool heater, fire pit, outdoor kitchen, or detached structure
  • Invisible-fence / dog-fence wires
  • Existing irrigation lines or low-voltage landscape lighting installed by a previous contractor
  • Pool plumbing, pool electrical, and pool-equipment lines
  • Lines to outbuildings, sheds, garages, casitas, or guest houses
  • Septic lines and aerobic system distribution lines
  • Water lines to outdoor showers, hose bibs added by previous owners, or irrigation drip lines installed without a contractor

Husky is not liable for damage to private utility lines that are not marked, flagged, or otherwise disclosed in writing before work begins. If you do not know where these lines are, please tell us in writing before the install date so we can plan around the unknowns or, if necessary, hire a private utility locator at your option and expense.

Warranty exclusions

The warranty does not cover:

  • Freeze damage resulting from the homeowner's failure to properly winterize the system. Texas freezes can crack PVC, valves, backflow preventers, and brass fittings. We will train you on winterization at handoff and provide written instructions; freeze damage caused by skipping winterization is not a warranty claim.
  • Damage caused by third parties — landscapers, fence installers, pool builders, foundation contractors, dig-it-yourself projects, or anyone other than Husky digging in or near the system after install.
  • Changes to the property after install — additions, demolitions, regrading, new fencing, or major landscape alterations that disturb buried lines or alter coverage requirements.
  • Normal wear and tear, including UV degradation of exposed nozzle bodies, mineral buildup from local water, and wear on moving parts beyond their expected service life.
  • Issues caused by water pressure outside the design range — pressure spikes from city service, low pressure caused by a partially closed main valve, or pressure variation caused by simultaneous heavy household use. We design the system for the pressure measured at the time of install; subsequent changes are outside our control.
  • Acts of God: floods, hail, lightning strikes, falling trees, vehicle impact, or vandalism.
  • Cosmetic settling of trenched soil over the first season, which is normal and expected.
