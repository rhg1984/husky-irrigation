Local expertise

Dallas yards need a Dallas irrigator.

Dallas clay. Our soil holds water, swells when wet, and cracks when dry. That wrecks shallow-buried lines over time and makes head-placement math different than the DIY video you watched. We trench deeper and account for seasonal movement.

Texas heat. July and August can put 100+ degrees on a lawn for weeks. We design with matched precipitation rates so every square foot gets the same water — no brown patches, no swampy corners — and we program smart controllers for cycle-and-soak to beat runoff.

Water pressure realities. North Texas municipal pressure averages 55–70 psi, but it varies by street and time of day. We measure at your hose bib before we spec the system, size the main accordingly, and add a pressure regulator if the reading demands it.
