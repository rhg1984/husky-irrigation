The six steps

From first walk-through to year-one tune-up.

  1. Day 1 · 30–60 min on-site

    Free on-site quote

    Rigo walks the yard with you. We measure the lot, ask about your turf and beds, find your hose-bib pressure, and sketch zones on the spot. Bring questions — we'd rather catch them now than after the trencher arrives.

    1
  2. 2
    Within 48 hours

    Written proposal

    You'll get an itemized PDF emailed to you — zone count, head count, controller model, drip runs, permit fees, install timeline. No "miscellaneous" line items, no upsells you didn't discuss. Sleep on it as long as you need.

  3. 2–5 business days

    City of Dallas permit

    Once you say go, we file the irrigation permit with the city. We pay the fee (it's in the proposal), submit our license info, and schedule the post-install inspection. You don't lift a finger.

    3
  4. 4
    1–3 days on-site

    Install

    Crew arrives early. Trenching, mainline tap, zone valves, lateral lines, heads, drip, controller, backflow. We hand-dig anywhere we'd risk roots and lay landscape staple-mat over trenches before backfill. End of day, your yard is rolled, watered, and walkable.

  5. Final day · 30 min

    Programming + walk-through

    We set seasonal schedules, teach you the controller (Rachio, Hunter Hydrawise, or your pick), label every valve box, and run each zone in front of you. You leave knowing how to pause, adjust, and seasonally tune the system yourself.

    5
  6. 6
    Year one · free

    Year-one adjustments

    Lawns settle, beds shift, you grow new turf. Any time in your first year, we'll come back to fine-tune head angles, swap a misting nozzle, or add a drip emitter — at no charge. Two-year parts-and-labor warranty after that.

Common questions.

Start to finish — how long does it really take?

expand_more

From the day you say "go," figure roughly 2 weeks. Permit takes 2–5 business days, then we schedule the install (most yards are 1–3 days on-site). Quotes themselves are usually scheduled within the first 2–3 business days of you reaching out.

Will my water bill go up?

expand_more

A well-designed smart system usually costs less than hand-watering, because it only runs when it needs to and avoids the runoff a hose creates. Expect a noticeable bump only if you were previously not watering at all. We program seasonal schedules to keep usage in check.

Are permit fees extra?

expand_more

No — they're an itemized line in your written proposal, which is your final price. We pay the city directly so there's nothing for you to handle.

How and when do I pay?

expand_more

A small deposit at signing covers permit and material order, the balance is due on completion. We accept check, ACH, Zelle, or major credit cards. No financing tricks, no surprise change orders.

Do you handle winterization?

expand_more

In Dallas, full blow-outs are usually unnecessary — we install drainable backflows and insulate exposed components. We'll show you a 5-minute pre-freeze checklist at the walk-through and you'll be set.
